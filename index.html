When numbers can't be represented with enough bits you can get roundoff errors. Unit 2: Computer Systems & Networks

The internet works because of computing devices connected together in a computer network. Messages are split into small packets, which travel along different paths.

Devices follow protocols such as TCP (reliable) or UDP (fast). Routers forward packets; DNS turns friendly URLs into numeric addresses.

The network is designed for redundancy and fault tolerance, making it scalable enough to support billions of devices. HTTP powers the World Wide Web (WWW).

Fiber Optics vs. Satellite Internet

Fiber Optics vs Satellite Internet, something many people should know for importance in different situations. Fiber Optic internet is used through cables, which allows it to provide a fast speed, a lower latency and higher reliability compared to satellite internet, it is also ideal when using multiple devices. However Satellite internet provides wireless internet access by using satellites to send and communicate through signals to provide internet. It is better and useful in remote rural areas where cable (or fiber) isnâ€™t available. But what are their true differences, which one is better? Optic Fiber Cable has a faster speed since it uses physical cables to transmit data, however Satellite uses signals to travel, which makes it slower and causes delay, and a higher amount of latency. However, Fiber is also more reliable, since it is unaffected by weather and blockages, satellites, suffers from rain, outages, and clouds. One thing fiber cannot do, is that the satellite works everywhere, it covers the whole planet, in places where fiber is not available, which can be beneficial for many to access the internet. Also, fiber costs more to install, satellite costs none, but it has higher service fees and caps on the amount of data used. To conclude, satellite is better for remote areas, where fiber is not located, but fiber is much better for at-home use, being faster, having less latency, and finally, being cheaper.
