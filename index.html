Unit 2: Computer Systems & Networks

The internet works because of computing devices connected in a computer network. Messages are split into packets, and each packet may follow a different path to reach its destination. To keep things organized, devices follow protocols like Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) for reliable delivery or User Datagram Protocol (UDP) when speed is more important than accuracy. Along the way, routers forward data and the Domain Name System (DNS) translates easy-to-remember URLs into IP addresses. Thanks to fault tolerance and redundancy, the internet keeps working even when parts fail. Itâ€™s also highly scalable, supporting billions of devices. Finally, HTTP makes it possible for us to browse the World Wide Web every day.
